This project direction focuses on simulation engines that connect genetic designs with spatial community behavior. The long-term goal is to test engineered organisms in increasingly realistic digital environments before moving toward deployment.

## Technical direction

The work begins with bacterial communities and agent-based models, then expands to host-associated microbiomes and plant-associated systems. Genetic designs can be exported through intermediate layers such as SimBOL and connected with simulation engines such as gro or CellModeller.

## Biological questions

Digital twins can help ask how engineered organisms invade, persist, communicate, report state, or orchestrate a community toward beneficial outputs. This is relevant to bacteria-as-medicine, gut and skin microbiome applications, resilient plant systems, and synthetic ecology.

## Early milestones

Early milestones include a model interface between genetic designs and simulation environments, bacterial consortium examples, and content examples that explain how simulation results connect back to DBTL workflows.
