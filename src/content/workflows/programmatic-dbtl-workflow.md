The programmatic DBTL workflow is the practical expression of the DRAGGON Lab platform: design with model-aware tools, build with traceable automation, test with calibrated measurements, and learn from standardized data.

## Stages

1. Design genetic networks and biological functions with LOICA and standards-backed design representations.
2. Build engineered organisms and samples using PUDU and BuildCompiler-style assembly, transformation, and plating workflows.
3. Test organisms with calibrated, metadata-rich measurements stored through Flapjack-style workflows.
4. Learn from the resulting datasets using SeqTrainer, graph models, sequence models, and hybrid modeling approaches.
5. Reuse designs, metadata, and data through SynBioSuite, SBOL, and SynBioHub-style infrastructure.

## Why it matters

A programmatic workflow makes each stage explicit enough to automate and inspect. It also makes collaboration easier because design decisions, build choices, experimental context, and learning outputs remain connected.
