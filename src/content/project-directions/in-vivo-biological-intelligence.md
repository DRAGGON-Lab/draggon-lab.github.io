This project direction explores how artificial intelligence ideas can be implemented inside living systems using genetic and genomic networks. Instead of treating cells only as factories, the lab asks how biological systems can sense, compute, decide, and act in useful ways.

## Research questions

How can biological transfer functions approximate computational functions? Which genetic parts and network motifs are robust enough to implement dynamic signals or reservoir-like behavior? How should DNA, RNA, proteins, chemicals, and environmental signals be represented as computational nodes and edges?

## Near-term targets

Near-term targets include phase-based and noise-robust genetic networks, reservoir computing with characterized parts, and designs that can connect biochemical sensing with useful reporting or actuation.

## Long-term vision

The long-term vision is to design intelligent organisms and communities for medicine, agriculture, environmental monitoring, and closed-loop interactions with machines.
