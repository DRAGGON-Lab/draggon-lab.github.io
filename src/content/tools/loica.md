LOICA is the design side of the DRAGGON platform ecosystem. It supports programmatic genetic network design, simulation, and characterization, helping researchers move from desired biological function to concrete network designs that can be built and tested.

## Role in the ecosystem

LOICA connects composition-to-function design with DBTL workflows. It can support mechanistic reasoning over genetic parts, genes, and network architectures, then connect those designs to downstream build planning and experimental characterization.

## Modeling scope

The platform direction includes stochastic simulation for single-cell behavior, ordinary differential equation models for growing populations in wells or bioreactors, partial differential equation models for colonies, and links to agent-based models through SimBOL-style interfaces.

## Why it matters

By making genetic network design programmatic and model-aware, LOICA gives the lab a route to dynamic signals, phase-based networks, reservoir computing, and biological implementations of computational architectures.
